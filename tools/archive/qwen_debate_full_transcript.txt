### 2. **Kelly Criterion and Optimal Bet Size**
The Kelly Criterion suggests that the optimal bet size is given by:
\[ f^* = \frac{bp - q}{b} \]
where:
- \( f^* \) is the fraction of the bankroll to bet,
- \( b \) is the net odds received on the bet (reward-to-risk ratio),
- \( p \) is the probability of winning,
- \( q \) is the probability of losing (1 - \( p \)).

For your parameters:
- \( b = 4 \)
- \( p = 0.55 \)
- \( q = 0.45 \)

\[ f^* = \frac{4 \times 0.55 - 0.45}{4} = \frac{2.2 - 0.45}{4} = \frac{1.75}{4} = 0.4375 \]
