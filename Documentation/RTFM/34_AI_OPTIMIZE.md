## What It Actually Does

When you click the **✨ AI Optimize** button on a profile, here's what happens behind the scenes:

1. **Reads your symbols** — It looks at every ticker in the profile (EUR_USD, BTCUSD, XAUUSD, etc.) and figures out *what* you're trading: forex, crypto, metals, stocks, or some chaos mix of all four.

2. **Analyzes market characteristics** — Different asset classes need different approaches. Forex has sessions and spreads. Crypto has 24/7 volatility. Metals are slow movers with sudden spikes. The AI knows this.

3. **Picks your strategies** — It assigns the best strategy *per asset class*, not just one blanket strategy. Your crypto gets a crypto-optimized strategy. Your forex gets the Conductor. Your gold doesn't get a scalper because gold doesn't scalp.

4. **Tunes your safety shields** — ATR Armor, Drawdown Breaker, Session Lockout, Leverage Sentry — there are 15+ safety guards. The AI enables the right ones and sets appropriate thresholds. Conservative for forex, looser for crypto (because crypto needs room to breathe or it'll get stopped out every 3 minutes).

5. **Configures performance modes** — Trailing stops, pyramiding depth, profit targets. The AI picks modes that match your asset mix.

6. **Sets up trend detection** — Which trend indicators should be active? EMA crossovers? MACD? ADX? The AI enables the right combination for your specific markets.

7. **Saves everything** — All changes go directly into your config. No clipboard. No copy-paste. No "remember to also change that other thing." It just works.

## Where to Find It

1. Open the **Profiles** tab (the sidebar with your trading profiles)
2. Select any profile — click on its name in the left panel
3. Right there at the top: a big shiny button that says **✨ AI Optimize**
4. Click it. Wait 5-10 seconds. Done.

That's it. That's the tutorial.

## What Gets Configured

The AI doesn't just pick a strategy and call it a day. It configures **six entire categories** of settings:

### 🎯 Strategy Selection
Which strategy runs on which asset class. The AI considers your specific symbols — is it EUR/USD? It knows that needs a session-aware forex strategy. Is it BTCUSD? It knows crypto needs 24/7 coverage. Mixed portfolio? Each asset class gets its own strategy.

### ⚙️ Strategy Workshop
The tuning knobs *within* the strategy. Risk per trade percentage. Risk-reward ratio. Pyramiding depth and risk scaling. Hold time limits. These are the numbers most people either leave at defaults (bad) or randomly change until something breaks (worse).

### 🛡️ Safety & Shields
The full defensive stack. Which safety guards are on, which are off, and what their trigger thresholds are. The AI is opinionated here — it tends toward protective defaults because, and I cannot stress this enough, **not blowing up is more important than maximizing gains.**

![chad](img/chad.png) **Chad:** "The AI turned on 11 safety shields for my crypto profile. I counted. I was using three. THREE. I was basically trading in a bathrobe on a highway."

### 📈 Performance & Profits
Trailing stops, breakeven trails, performance modes (Sniper, Regime Sync, House Money, etc.). These are the "how do I keep my profits" settings that separate accounts that grow from accounts that give everything back.

### 📊 Trend Detection
Which trend indicators to activate. The AI picks a combination that's appropriate for your markets — heavier trend detection for forex (which trends in waves), lighter or different indicators for crypto (which trends like a drunk person on a skateboard).

### 💰 Risk Parameters
Position sizing, max exposure, daily loss limits. The stuff that determines whether a bad day costs you 2% or 40%.

## The History Panel

After AI Optimize runs, you'll see a detailed breakdown card showing:

- **When** it was last run
- **What** it changed, with per-category explanations
- **Why** it made each recommendation

This history persists. When you come back tomorrow and select the same profile, you'll see your last optimization with all the reasoning intact. If you want to re-run it (maybe you added new symbols), just click the button again.

## Common Questions

### "Does it overwrite my manual settings?"
Yes. That's the point. If you've manually tuned something and want to keep it, don't click AI Optimize on that profile. Create a separate profile for experimentation — profiles are free.

### "Can I undo it?"
Not with a single click, but the history panel shows you exactly what was changed. You can manually revert any setting. Or just keep a "backup" profile with your old settings.

### "How does it pick strategies?"
It knows every strategy's backtest performance, asset class compatibility, and risk profile. It cross-references this with your symbol list. If you're trading EUR_USD and GBP_USD? You're getting the Conductor or Supply & Demand — proven forex winners. If you're trading BTCUSD and ETHUSD? Probably RoboCop or RSI/MACD — crypto-optimized strategies.

### "What if I have symbols from multiple asset classes?"
That's actually its favorite scenario. It assigns a *different* strategy per asset class. Your crypto symbols get one strategy, your forex symbols get another, and everything plays nicely together because that's literally what the bot was built to do.

![chad](img/chad.png) **Chad:** "I put BTCUSD, EUR_USD, XAUUSD, and SPY in one profile just to see what would happen. It gave me four different strategies and a safety config that somehow made sense. I felt like I was watching a surgeon operate."

### "Is it using ChatGPT?"
It uses whatever AI model you've configured (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, etc. — configurable via the AI Model setting). The important thing is that it receives a massive system prompt containing every strategy's description, backtest results, and configuration options. It's not guessing — it's choosing from a curated menu with all the performance data attached.

### "Should I run it every time I change my symbols?"
If you add or remove an entire asset class (e.g., adding crypto to a forex-only profile), yes. If you just added one more forex pair, probably not necessary.

## Pro Tips

1. **Make a test profile first** — Duplicate your current profile, run AI Optimize on the copy, and compare. Don't yolo it on your live profile at 2 AM.

2. **Read the reasoning** — The history panel doesn't just show what changed, it shows *why*. The AI's reasoning is often educational. You'll learn things about your own strategy you didn't know.

3. **Don't fight the AI** — If it turns something off that you had on, there's usually a reason. Check the reasoning before reflexively turning it back on. 

4. **Re-run periodically** — Markets change. Your symbol list changes. What was optimal 3 months ago might not be optimal now. Re-running AI Optimize after major portfolio changes keeps your config fresh.

5. **Compare profiles** — Run AI Optimize on your crypto profile and your forex profile separately. Notice how the configurations are completely different. That's the whole point — one size does NOT fit all.
