## The Three States

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/professor.png" width="150"></td><td><b>PROFESSOR</b>:<br>"The card has exactly three states. Think of them as traffic lights."</td></tr></table>

### 🔒 LOCKED — "Sit On Your Hands"

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Your trades are still open. Money is still floating. You haven't won or lost anything yet — the game is still in progress.<br><br>This is like being at a poker table in the middle of a hand. You don't get up and go to the ATM mid-hand. You don't cash out chips you're still playing with. Sit there. Wait. Let the trades resolve. When the dust settles, the card will tell you what to do.<br><br>Withdrawing capital while trades are open is one of the dumbest things you can do because it artificially spikes your margin utilization. You're pulling the floor out from under your own open positions. Don't do that."</td></tr></table>

### 🛡️ SHIELDED — "We're Under Water. Protect the Base."

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"You're in the red. Your PnL for the selected timeframe is negative. The bot isn't making you money right now — it's <em>recovering.</em><br><br>This is NOT the time to withdraw. This is the time to let the bot work. The shields are up. The compounding machine needs every dollar it has to climb back to the high-water mark. You don't pull soldiers off the front line during a battle. You send MORE.<br><br>When you see SHIELDED, the card is saying: 'Don't touch anything. I know you're nervous. I know you want to pull the money and run. Don't. We've been here before and we came back. Let the math work.'"</td></tr></table>

### 💰 CASHOUT — "You Got Paid. Here's How Much to Take."

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"THIS is the one you want to see. This is the green light. The card is saying: 'You made money. Real money. Closed trades. Confirmed profit. Here is how much of it you should physically withdraw from your brokerage and move to your bank account <em>right now.</em>'<br><br>And it doesn't tell you to take everything. It tells you to take the RIGHT amount."</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/chad.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CHAD</b>:<br>"Why not take all of it?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Because if you take ALL the profit out, your account stays the same size forever. It never grows. You're working a <em>job</em>, not building a <em>machine.</em><br><br>If you take NONE of it, your account grows but you never see a dime. You're building a machine you never benefit from. You're the hamster who built a nicer wheel but still never leaves the cage.<br><br>The sweet spot is in the middle. Take <em>some</em> for yourself — that's your paycheck, that's your reward, that's you going to a restaurant and ordering something besides water. Leave the rest in the account so it compounds. The machine gets bigger. Next month's paycheck gets bigger. The month after that? Even bigger.<br><br>That's the whole game. That's the system. Pay yourself AND grow the machine."</td></tr></table>

## The Math: 50% vs. 75%

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/professor.png" width="150"></td><td><b>PROFESSOR</b>:<br>"The card uses two tiers based on velocity — how fast you made the money relative to your capital."</td></tr></table>

| Situation | Recommended Pull | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Steady grind** (under 2.5% return in selected timeframe) | **50%** of profit | Normal earnings. Take half as your paycheck. Leave half to compound. This is the 'salary' model. Predictable. Sustainable. |
| **Volatility spike** (over 2.5% return in selected timeframe) | **75%** of profit | Anomaly money. The market gave you a gift and gifts don't repeat on demand. Take 75% off the table because this pace is NOT sustainable and you shouldn't compound money that was partly lucky. |

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Let me make this real. You've got a $10,000 account. You made $300 this week on the '7D' timeframe. That's a 3% return — above the 2.5% threshold. The card lights up and says: <b>$225.</b><br><br>That means: go to your brokerage, initiate a withdrawal of $225, and put it in your bank account. Your trading account goes from $10,300 to $10,075. You still grew your capital by $75. AND you have $225 in your pocket. You got paid AND the machine got bigger.<br><br>Next week you make $250 at a normal pace — below 2.5%. The card says take 50%: <b>$125.</b> Your account grows from $10,075 to $10,200. You pocket $125.<br><br>After two weeks you have $350 in the bank and your account grew by $200. That's the system. That's the compound effect with a built-in paycheck."</td></tr></table>
