## 🏓 1. Forex and Crypto (The "Ping-Pong" Markets)

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Forex and Crypto spend 80% of their lives trapped in a box. They bounce between a ceiling and a floor over and over again. We call this 'ranging.' It's a never-ending game of ping-pong between central banks trying to keep their currencies stable."</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"Oh, I love ping-pong! So how do we trade it?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"With **Mean Reversion** or the **Forex Hybrid Scalper**. You don't expect the ball to fly out of the room. You just wait for it to hit the wall, and you bet that it's going to bounce back. You set a tiny `0.5R` profit target. You take your $28, you put it in your pocket, and you wait for the next bounce."</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"What if I use the Trend Rider instead? What if I hold out for a massive 10R win?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Then you are the guy standing in the middle of a ping-pong table hoping the ball decides to just keep flying in a straight line forever. The ball will hit the wall, reverse, and smack you right in the face. Take your small profits and run."</td></tr></table>

## 🚢 2. Stocks and ETFs (The "Slow & Steady" Markets)

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"Okay, so what about Apple? And the S&P 500? Are they ping-pong balls too?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"No. Stocks are massive cruise ships. They are pushed by the retirement funds of millions of angry boomers. It takes an incredible amount of energy to turn them around. But once they start moving in a direction, they *keep* moving in that direction for weeks. Or months."</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"So... we use the Trend Rider here?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Exactly! You use the **Trend Rider** or the **London Sweep**. When the cruise ship leaves the port, you don't jump off halfway through the trip for a tiny `0.5R` profit. You strap yourself to the deck, you let the trailing stop do the work, and you ride that trend for 3R, 5R, or 10R until the ship finally runs out of fuel."</td></tr></table>

## 🚨 3. Precious Metals / Gold (The "Panic" Market)

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"Gold is shiny. I want to trade Gold. I bet Gold is a cruise ship too!"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"Gold is the financial equivalent of a fire alarm. Most of the time, it just sits on the wall doing absolutely nothing. For days. Weeks, even. It will chop up your Trend Rider and spit it out."</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/rookie.png" width="150"></td><td><b>ROOKIE</b>:<br>"So we don't trade it?"</td></tr></table>

<table><tr><td width="170"><img src="img/creator.png" width="150"></td><td><b>CREATOR</b>:<br>"We trade it when someone pulls the alarm. When a central bank makes a surprise announcement, or someone drops a bomb, Gold wakes up in a sheer panic and explodes in one direction. We use Breakout strategies for this, like the **New York Drive**. We draw a tight box around Gold while it's sleeping, and the exact millisecond it violently breaks out of the box, we grab on and ride the explosion."</td></tr></table>
