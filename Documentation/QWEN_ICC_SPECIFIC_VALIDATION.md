# Qwen's Validation of ICC (Indication, Correction, Continuation) Framework

**Date:** 2025-12-28
**Framework:** Trade by SCI's ICC (Indication, Correction, Continuation)

## Question:

In the ICC framework:
- **Indication** = break of most recent swing high/low on HTF
- **Correction** = pullback after indication, ideally with liquidity sweep
- **Continuation** = break of correction structure + close beyond correction range (entry trigger)

Are liquidity sweeps during the **Correction phase** a MANDATORY requirement for entering a **Continuation** trade, or are they PROBABILISTIC INDICATORS that improve setup quality?

## Qwen's Response (Word-for-Word):

Great question — this is a common point of confusion among traders learning the **ICC (Indication, Correction, Continuation)** methodology as taught by **Trade by SCI**.

### ✅ **Answer:**
**Liquidity sweeps during the Correction phase are PROBABILISTIC INDICATORS, not a MANDATORY requirement** for entering a Continuation trade.

---

### 🔍 **Explanation:**

In the **ICC framework**, the **Correction** phase is not just a simple pullback — it's a **structured correction** that often includes **liquidity sweeps**. These sweeps are **highly desirable** because they:

- **Confirm the strength of the move** (i.e., the market is not just pulling back, but is testing and retesting key levels).
- **Provide confluence** with **volume**, **price action**, and **order flow**.
- **Increase the probability** of a **successful Continuation**.

However, **they are not a strict requirement** for entry. The **Continuation** is triggered by:

> **"Break of correction structure + close beyond correction range"**

This is the **entry trigger**, not the **setup quality**.

---

### 📌 **Key Takeaways from Trade by SCI:**

1. **Liquidity sweeps are a **high-probability** feature of a strong Correction**, but not a **mandatory** one.
2. **You can still take a Continuation trade** even if the Correction phase lacks a liquidity sweep — it just means the **setup is lower confluence** (e.g., B-grade vs A+).
3. **The decision to trade is based on the **trigger**, not the **setup quality**.**
4. **Risk management and trade execution** are more important than perfect setup quality.

---

### 📊 **Example:**

- **Indication** = Break of HTF swing high on 4H chart.
- **Correction** = Pullback to 50% Fibonacci or previous support, but **no liquidity sweep**.
- **Continuation** = Break of correction structure (e.g., a higher low or a bullish pattern) + close beyond correction range.
- **Entry** = Triggered, even without a liquidity sweep — but with **lower confluence**.

---

### 🧠 **Trade by SCI Philosophy:**

> "The market doesn’t always give you perfect setups. You have to trade what you see, not what you want."

So, while **liquidity sweeps are ideal**, they are **not required**. You can still **trade the Continuation** with **lower confluence**, as long as the **trigger is valid** and **risk is managed**.

---

### 📌 Final Recommendation:

- **Do not reject Continuation entries just because there was no liquidity sweep** in the Correction.
- **Use liquidity sweeps as a **confluence enhancer**, not a gatekeeper.**
- **Focus on the trigger** and **risk-reward** when deciding whether to enter.
